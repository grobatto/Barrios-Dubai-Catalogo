## LEVEL 2: CATEGORIES (Uruguay Collection Only)

The Uruguay collection is organized into 7 professional categories based on form, mounting style, and geological type.

### Category 1: SHAPES

**Directory:** `uruguay/torres/`, `uruguay/piramides/`, `uruguay/huevos/`, `uruguay/cortados/`, `uruguay/freeform-a/`, `uruguay/freeform-b/`, etc.

**Description:** Pieces with distinctive geometric or natural forms

**Subcategories:**
1. **Torres (Towers)**
   - Tall, vertically-oriented crystal towers
   - Dramatic height-to-width ratio
   - Perfect for display shelves
   - Count: ~15 products

2. **Piramides (Pyramids)**
   - Geometric pyramid shapes
   - Symmetrical formations
   - Educational value
   - Count: ~12 products

3. **Huevos (Eggs)**
   - Smooth, egg-shaped formations
   - Natural polished surfaces
   - Decorative appeal
   - Count: ~8 products

4. **Cortados (Cut Pieces)**
   - Flat cut surfaces
   - Professional finishing
   - Geological cross-sections visible
   - Count: ~7 products

5. **Freeforms (Natural Forms)**
   - Uncut natural formations
   - Artistic geological shapes
   - Collectors' favorites
   - Count: ~10 products

**Naming Convention:**
- `[Type]-[Number]` (e.g., `torres-01`, `piramides-05`)
- Professional descriptive names (e.g., "Purple Harmony Tower", "Natural Lavender Pyramid")
- All names reviewed and refined by AI analysis

**Image Standards:**
- Minimum: 1 image per product
- Optimal: 2 images (front and top/angle view)
- Premium pieces: 3+ images

**Display:**
- Individual product cards
- Hover effects with scale animation
- 1x1 grid on desktop, single column on mobile

## NAMING CONVENTIONS

### Professional Naming System (AI-Refined)

All products have professional, descriptive names refined through AI analysis to meet international catalog standards.

### Naming Levels

#### Level 1: Product Type
Describes geological form or mounting style
- Examples: "Tower", "Geode", "Cluster", "Mounted Piece"

#### Level 2: Descriptive Adjective
Adds personality and market appeal
- Color-based: "Deep Purple", "Royal Violet", "Lavender"
- Form-based: "Cathedral", "Dream", "Majesty"
- Origin-based: "Uruguayan", "Artigas"

#### Level 3: Collection/Category
Indicates broader grouping
- "Grand Collection" for premium pieces
- Category name for Uruguay collection items

### Examples

**Grand Collection:**
- "Majestic Cathedral Geode - Grand #01"
- "Royal Purple Statement Geode - Grand #02"
- "The Grand Amethyst Cathedral - Grand #09"

**Uruguay/Shapes:**
- "Purple Harmony Tower"
- "Natural Lavender Pyramid"
- "Elegant Crystal Heart"

**Uruguay/With Base:**
- "Purple Tower on Brass Base"
- "Deep Violet Cluster on Wood Stand"

**Uruguay/Amethyst:**
- "Deep Purple Cluster"
- "Raw Amethyst Formation"

**Uruguay/Agates:**
- "Banded Agate Slab - Burgundy"
- "Natural Agate Slice"

### Naming Guidelines for New Products

When adding new products to the catalog:

1. **Identify the Category**
   - Which type does this product belong to?
   - What is its primary characteristic?

2. **Create Level 1: Product Type**
   - Tower, Geode, Cluster, Piece, etc.
   - Clear geological or form description

3. **Create Level 2: Descriptive Adjective**
   - Color: Deep Purple, Royal Violet, Lavender, etc.
   - Form: Cathedral, Crystal, Elegant, Natural, etc.
   - Quality: Premium, Museum, Exceptional, Rare, etc.

4. **Create Level 3: Additional Specification (if needed)**
   - Size: Large, Medium, Small
   - Material: Pure Amethyst, Agate, Mixed
   - Base: if mounted on stand

5. **Final Name Format**
   ```
   [Adjective] [Type Name] - [Category]
   Example: "Deep Purple Cathedral Geode - Grand Collection"
   ```

6. **Validate**
   - Is it professional and elegant?
   - Does it market the product effectively?
   - Is it consistent with existing names?
   - Would an international buyer understand it?

## DATABASE MAINTENANCE

### Adding New Products

1. **Photograph & Catalog** - Take professional photos, store in appropriate directory
2. **Create Entry** - Add JSON object to products.json
3. **Update Category** - Assign to appropriate category
4. **Name Product** - Follow professional naming convention
5. **Verify Images** - Ensure all image paths are correct
6. **Update Documentation** - Add to appropriate guide (IMAGE_ANALYSIS_REPORT.md, ROTATION_LOG.md)
7. **Test** - Verify display in catalog, lightbox, and responsive views
8. **Commit** - Git commit with descriptive message
9. **Deploy** - Rebuild and deploy to production

### Moving Products Between Collections

If a standard product should become Grand:
1. Update `collection` field from "standard" to "grand"
2. Update `section` field to "grandes"
3. Add more detailed dimensions/weight if not present
4. Update quality field to "Museum" or "Premium"
5. Add more images for comprehensive gallery
6. Update in IMAGE_ANALYSIS_REPORT.md
7. Test thoroughly in catalog
8. Commit and deploy
